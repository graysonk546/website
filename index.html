Can Loading Hopper

The Can Loading Hopper is the can stacking and organizing subsystem of a wheeled can collecting robot. The purpose of the robot is to collect and position 6 horizontal drink cans so that they can be deposited veritcally.

The hopper takes advantage of coupled rotary motion to load cans, and implements a belt-driven linear slide to move cans vertically.

Can Collecting Robot V1

The goal with V1 of the can-collecting robot was to autonomously navigate a competition surface, picking up horizontal drink cans along the way. At the end of the competition run, the cans were to be flipped vertically and dispensed in a 2x3 grid formation to a precision of ±5mm.

To achieve this, our team opted for encoded rollers as means of can intake, and a uni-axially rotating hopper to position cans horizontally before flipping 90° to dispense them.

Optimized Engine Cycle

The Brayton Cycle is the thermodynamic process behind the operation of many gas turbine and jet engines. To increase the efficiency of this process, compressors can be added repurpose lost heat.

The goal of the thermodynamic simulation was to find the limit of efficiency as the number of compressors approaches infinity. At the end of the investigation it was determined that the Ericsson Cycle is approached in this limit, which exhibits Carnot efficiency (maximum efficiency)!

Skateboarding with LM

The goal of this physics simulation was to model the periodic behaviour of a skateboarder riding a half-pipe. Of particular interest was precise pumping motion required to minimize the time taken to reach the top of the ramp.

To model the situation, a skateboarder on a ramp was approximated by a mass-spring system traversing a semi-circular path. Lagrangian Mechanics (LM), a formulation of classical mechanics, was used to characterize the motion of the system and Python was used to investigate and visualize it.
